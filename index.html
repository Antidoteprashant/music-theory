Musical Notes

Music is written using a set of letters called the musical alphabet. These letters represent different pitches.

  • The musical alphabet consists of 7 letters: A, B, C, D, E, F, G.
  • After G, we go back to A (but at a higher pitch).
  • Each repetition is called an octave.
  • Notes can be modified with sharps (#) and flats (b).

Major Scales

A scale is a sequence of notes played in a specific order, usually going up or down in pitch.

The most common scale is the Major Scale. It sounds happy and bright.

Example: C Major Scale

C D E F G A B C
  • Notice there are no sharps (#) or flats (b) in C Major!
  • This is why C Major is often taught first to beginners.

Chords

A chord is when you play multiple notes at the same time. Chords provide the harmony for a song.

Common Types:

Major

Sounds happy and bright

C + E + G
Minor

Sounds sad or serious

A + C + E
